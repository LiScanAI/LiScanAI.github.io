   Open-source AI-specific licenses represent a new form of social contract in the digital age, introducing complexities and multidimensional challenges for license compatibility detection. Unlike traditional SPDX licenses, open-source AI models adopt a hierarchical licensing framework encompassing global licenses and component licenses (Code, Weights, Data), along with novel clauses such as technical constraints, commercial restrictions, and ethical responsibilities. These unique characteristics render conventional license compatibility detection tools ineffective.
   This paper introduces LiScan.AI, the first LLM-powered framework detecting license incompatibilities for AI artifacts through: (1) License-aware Model Derivation (LMD) Graphs modeling cross-model dependencies, (2) legal-validated SPDX/AI license term taxonomies, and (3) LLM-powered semantic analysis of unstructured text. It identifies conflicts in standard obligations and AI-specific constraints (technical/commercial/ethical), integrating insights from evolving AI licensing paradigms.
   Our evaluation results show that LiScan.AI significantly outperforms existing approaches (achieving 89.7% Precision and 87.8% Recall) in detecting license incompatibilities. Our analysis of 1,528,095 models reveals widespread issues, with 37.5% of global-component license pairs and 80.7% of derivative models showing incompatibilities. In real-world validation, we reported 114 issues detected by LiScan.AI to Hugging Face developers, receiving responses on 35 cases - with 28 (80.0%) confirming the identified incompatibilities. This demonstrates LiScan.AI's practical effectiveness in raising awareness and verifying compliance risks in the AI ecosystem.
